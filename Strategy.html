STRATEGIES & THREATS

Layered Defense Architecture

Very Short Range: MANPADS and Counter-UAS systems (0-6 km) defend against low-flying drones and missiles.

Short Range: SPYDER and QRSAM (15-30 km) protect forward bases and mobile formations.

Medium Range: Akash and Barak-8 (30-100 km) shield airbases and strategic assets.

Long Range: S-400 and future Project Kusha (150-400+ km) provide outer-layer deterrence.

Emerging Aerial Threats

Drone swarms: Countered by electronic warfare and laser systems (D4, Bhargavastra).

Hypersonic missiles: Detection and interception via advanced radars and future interceptors.

Stealth aircraft: Multi-sensor fusion and AESA radars track low-RCS targets.

Operation Sindoor

Operation Sindoor, conducted on 2nd-3rd May 2025, was one of India's most significant air defense actions. It was triggered by rising border tensions following ceasefire violations and reported militant infiltration in the Poonch sector. In response, Pakistan launched a coordinated aerial assault aiming to overwhelm India's defenses and target airbases, radar sites, and ammunition depots in northern and western sectors.
The attack involved a saturation strike of over 520 aerial threats, including 300+ drones, 150+ cruise missiles, and around 70 decoy or EW UAVs. These threats were launched from PoK and Sindh, timed to arrive in waves. The strategy aimed to test India's air defense network and exploit any weaknesses or delay in its response.
India's reaction was swift, coordinated through the Integrated Air Command and Control System (IACCS). Within moments, threat alerts were issued. Data from ground radars, AWACS, and satellites like Cartosat and RISAT were fused in real time. IACCS prioritized threats, selected interceptors, and guided responses across the layered defense grid.
The defense systems activated in layers: SPYDER neutralized drones near forward areas, while QRSAM protected mobile units near Ganganagar. At medium range, Akash and Barak-8 intercepted incoming missiles targeting key assets. S-400 systems, deployed in depth, handled long-range, high-speed threats with great efficiency.
In total, over 480 of 520 threats were intercepted. SPYDER downed 140 drones, Akash stopped 110 missiles, Barak-8 engaged 85 targets, and S-400 took out over 100 threats—all with success rates above 90%. No major damage or casualties were reported. Although IAF jets were scrambled, ground systems handled the defense independently.
Operation Sindoor became a milestone in India's defense preparedness. It showcased the power of layered air defense and real-time threat management. Following the operation, India fast-tracked deployment of Directed Energy Weapons (DEWs) and improved coordination among the IAF, Army, and DRDO, reshaping its air defense doctrine.
