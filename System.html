KEY SYSTEM DEFENSE

Akash Missile System

  • The Akash is India’s first indigenously developed medium-range surface-to-air missile, developed by DRDO under IGMDP.

  • It has a range of 25–30 km and can hit targets at altitudes up to 20 km.

  • It uses the Rajendra phased-array radar for target tracking and guidance.

  • Akash is mounted on wheeled or tracked platforms for rapid redeployment.

  • Inducted by the IAF in 2014 and the Army in 2015, it has intercepted drones and cruise missiles in operations.

Barak - 8

  • Barak-8 is a joint project between DRDO and Israel Aerospace Industries for medium- to long-range air defense.

  • It has a range of 70–100 km and features a 360° AESA radar-based active seeker.

  • The land-based MRSAM was inducted into the Navy in 2016 and the Army in 2025.

  • It can intercept aircraft, helicopters, UAVs, and anti-ship missiles effectively.

  • Barak-8 showed successful multi-target engagement during Operation Sindoor.

SPYDER

  • SPYDER is a mobile short- to medium-range air defense system developed by Rafael and IAI.

  • It uses Python-5 (infrared) and Derby (radar-guided) missiles for interception.

  • The system covers a range of 15–35 km and can activate in under five seconds.

  • Truck-mounted, SPYDER offers rapid point-defense for high-value assets.

  • India procured it in 2009, and it successfully downed a Pakistani drone in 2019.

S - 400 Triumf

  • The S-400 Triumf is a long-range SAM system from Russia, known as 'Sudarshan Chakra' in India.

  • It can engage aerial targets up to 400 km away and at 30 km altitude.

  • The system uses various missile types to create a layered defense.

  • Each regiment includes two missile battalions and a total of 128 missiles.

  • India signed a $5.43 billion deal in 2018 and received 3 of 5 regiments by 2025.

Iron Dome

  • Iron Dome is an Israeli short-range air defense system designed to intercept rockets and artillery.

  • It uses Tamir interceptor missiles to neutralize threats within a 4–70 km range.

  • The system is optimized to defend densely populated areas against rocket barrages.

  • India considered co-developing Iron Dome but chose to pursue local alternatives like Project Kusha.
